Period Overview

June 1–25 is the first full reporting window with all three new formats live simultaneously: Google AI Max, Google Performance Max, and the Meta conversion-optimisation A/B test. The program delivered 650.8K impressions and 7,404 clicks on $5,009 across Google, Meta, and LinkedIn. California holds a 58% click-volume lead across all three channels. Meta generates the bulk of raw reach and clicks at the lowest CPC; LinkedIn delivers the highest CTR; Google's three-format mix covers search-intent candidates and is still in its algorithmic learning phase.

"Meta drives reach, LinkedIn drives intent, and Google covers the search-qualified tail — all three demand mechanisms are now active. The next lever is creative refresh on California Meta and scaling Instagram Reels, which is outperforming every other placement in the program."

Audience Signals — Age & Device

25–34 is the top age band by Search spend in both markets. Mobile phones dominate across Search and AI Max; Performance Max extends reach across mobile, desktop, TV screens, and tablets.

  • ✦ Search carries 75% of Google spend and drives the highest CTR at 5.71%. The audience reaching Two Chairs through keyword search is highly self-qualified. Washington's overall Google CTR (2.96%) leads California (2.41%) — consistent with the broader pattern of stronger WA audience alignment across the program.
  • ✦ Performance Max is functioning as the reach engine. PMax generated 79% of all Google impressions at $175 — the cheapest cost-per-impression in the program. The CA/WA click split (94 vs 35) mirrors the broader program geo weighting.
  • ✦ AI Max is showing early intent-level CTR at 7.00%. Across its first observed days the format is matching and exceeding Search on click-through rate. Volume is early-stage; the format is still building signal and performance trajectory is positive.

Market Read

California leads on click volume (58%) driven by a larger Meta impression base and higher Google allocation. However, Washington consistently delivers stronger CTR on both LinkedIn (2.72% vs 2.32%) and Meta (1.08% vs 0.75%) on near-identical spend ($2,452 vs $2,557). The efficiency gap is consistent across both channels — the WA audience is better aligned with current creative, and that signal is worth acting on before the next cycle.

06 · Next Steps
What the data is telling us
  • 01 · Meta
    Instagram Reels is the engagement leader
    Both markets are showing Reels as the top placement by CTR — CA at 3.21%, WA at 2.52%. The format is clearly connecting with the audience in a way the volume placements are not. The next cycle should lean into what's working here from a creative and placement standpoint.
  • 02 · LinkedIn
    Adjacent clinical titles are showing real engagement
    Physical Therapist, SLP, and OT are generating meaningful impressions and clicks alongside the primary Therapist titles. These audiences may respond better to messaging that speaks to their specific professional context rather than a generic therapist-recruitment angle.
  • 03 · LinkedIn
    Washington CTR leads California
    WA is delivering 2.72% CTR vs CA's 2.32%. The pattern mirrors what's happening on Meta — the WA audience is better aligned with current creative. Worth noting as a consistent signal across both channels.
  • 04 · Google
    Search is punching above its weight
    At 5.71% CTR, Search is the highest-performing format by engagement quality. The audience reaching Two Chairs through keyword search is highly self-qualified. This is worth keeping in view as the program matures and more signal accumulates across PMax and AI Max.
